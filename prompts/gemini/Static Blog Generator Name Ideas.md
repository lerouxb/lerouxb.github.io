Choosing a name for a static site generator (SSG) usually involves balancing two things: the **tech stack** it uses and the **vibe** of being fast, lightweight, and unmoving.

Here are a few ideas categorized by the "energy" they project:

### The "Fast & Light" Crowd

These names emphasize the speed of static files and the lack of a heavy backend.

- **BoltPress:** Simple, punchy, and implies speed.
- **SwiftScript:** Great if the generator is focused on a streamlined workflow.
- **Vapor:** Sounds ethereal and lightweight, as if the site barely exists on the server.
- **AeroStatic:** Suggests something aerodynamic and modern.

### The "Nature & Structure" Crowd

Many popular SSGs (like Hugo or Jekyll) use classic, sturdy names.

- **Cedar:** Sounds reliable, organic, and long-lasting.
- **Monolith:** A bit ironic for a small blog, but suggests something solid and unbreakable.
- **Strata:** Layers of content built up over time.
- **Quarry:** Where you "get the stones" to build your site.

### The "Tech & Dev" Crowd

These are a bit more "on the nose" for developers who like clean syntax.

- **Markdownic:** Perfect if the primary focus is writing in Markdown.
- **HydraSSG:** If the generator can output to multiple platforms at once.
- **NullStack:** A play on "Full Stack," implying there is no server-side stack to worry about.
- **GitGo:** Short, catchy, and implies a Git-based workflow.
